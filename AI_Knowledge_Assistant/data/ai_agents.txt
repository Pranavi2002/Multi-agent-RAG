AI Agents are systems that can perceive their environment, make decisions, and take actions to achieve a goal.

In modern AI systems, agents are often modular and can perform specialized tasks.

Examples of AI agents in a multi-agent system include:
- Document Fetcher Agent: loads and processes documents
- Embedding Agent: converts text into vector representations
- Vector Store Agent: stores and retrieves embeddings efficiently
- LLM Agent: generates responses using a language model
- Evaluation Agent: evaluates the quality of generated responses
- Observability Agent: logs system performance and metrics

Multi-agent architectures improve scalability, modularity, and debugging of AI systems.

They are commonly used in RAG systems, autonomous workflows, and AI orchestration frameworks.
